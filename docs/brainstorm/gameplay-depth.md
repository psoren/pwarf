# Gameplay Depth Brainstorm

What keeps players engaged in colony sims (RimWorld, Dwarf Fortress, etc) and what pwarf is missing.

## Threats that force decisions

- Raids/sieges that escalate over time (DF: goblin sieges, RW: raid scaling with wealth)
- Seasonal challenges (winter = no farming, need stockpiles)
- Resource depletion — the easy stuff runs out, you dig deeper or expand
- Events with tradeoffs ("a caravan offers 50 food for your best crafter")

## Player expression / building

- Room design that matters (bedrooms, dining halls, temples — quality affects mood)
- Defense planning (chokepoints, traps, walls, kill corridors)
- Workshop chains (ore → smelt → bars → forge → weapon)
- Base layout as a puzzle — traffic flow, stockpile placement, zoning

## Progression that changes how you play

- Tech/research trees unlocking new buildings and capabilities
- Deeper = rarer materials = better gear (vertical progression)
- Population milestones triggering new challenges (DF: monarch arrival)
- Seasons/years that shift what's possible

## Meaningful choices about people

- Job specialization vs generalization tradeoff
- Who gets the good gear, the good room
- Triage — who do you save when things go wrong
- Accepting/rejecting refugees (more hands but more mouths)

## Stories that emerge

- Named enemies that return
- Dwarves with grudges, rivalries, personal goals
- Artifacts with history
- "The fortress fell because..." moments

## Current gaps in pwarf

- Monsters and combat exist, but threats don't escalate
- Skills exist but no reason to specialize
- Building exists but rooms don't matter beyond beds
- No production chain beyond "mine stone, build thing"
- No scarcity that forces hard decisions
- No late-game progression or player-driven goals
