## Balance Issues

### 1. Resource exhaustion is inevitable and unrecoverable (CRITICAL)

Starting resources are consumed and never replenished sustainably. With 30 drink items and 7 dwarves:
- `DRINK_DECAY_PER_TICK = 0.2` means drink need drops from 80 to 30 (the interrupt threshold) in ~250 ticks
- Each drink restores 50 (`DRINK_RESTORE_AMOUNT`), but there are only 30 drink items total for 7 dwarves
- By tick ~400, all drinks are consumed. Dwarves hit drink=0 and die in 245 ticks (`DEHYDRATION_TICKS`)

The food situation is similar: `FOOD_DECAY_PER_TICK = 0.15`, items restore 40 (`FOOD_RESTORE_AMOUNT`).

**The core problem:** Auto-forage exists (`MIN_FORAGE_FOOD_STOCK = 5`) but does not generate drink. Auto-brew exists (`MIN_DRINK_STOCK = 10`) but may not trigger fast enough or produce enough. Dwarves appear to run out of drink items and die of dehydration in every scenario.

### 2. Monsters are too lethal for early game (HIGH)

- `MONSTER_SPAWN_INTERVAL = 200` means a monster arrives every 200 ticks (roughly 4 in-game days)
- `MONSTER_NIGHT_CREATURE_HEALTH = 50`, `MONSTER_ATTACK_BASE = 8` vs `DWARF_ATTACK_BASE = 6`
- Monsters deal significantly more damage than dwarves, and spawn relentlessly from tick 200 onward
- In every scenario, 3-4 of 7 dwarves die to monster attacks. Since dwarves have no military organization or equipment, each monster encounter is a coin flip for the targeted dwarf
- Dead dwarves reduce the labor pool, accelerating the food/drink crisis

### 3. Need decay is uniformly oppressive (MEDIUM)

At tick 200 (the first observation point), every dwarf in every scenario shows the same pattern:
- Food: 50 (low), Drink: 40 (low), Sleep: 56 (ok)
- Purpose: 42 (low), Beauty: 48 (low)

All six needs trend downward simultaneously. There is no "comfortable equilibrium" -- even with ample supplies, dwarves are always in mild distress. The beauty need (`BEAUTY_DECAY_PER_TICK = 0.03`, passive restore only `0.02`) steadily declines because there are no beauty structures in headless scenarios.

### 4. Starvation scenario: drink kills before hunger (MINOR)

The "starvation" scenario has 3 food / 20 drink. Despite the name, the first death is from a monster attack at tick ~300, not starvation. Dwarves that die of resource depletion die of dehydration (drink=0) before starvation (food=0), because `DRINK_DECAY_PER_TICK` (0.2) > `FOOD_DECAY_PER_TICK` (0.15) and `DEHYDRATION_TICKS` (245) < `STARVATION_TICKS` (490).

### 5. Day counter is wrong (BUG)

The sim reports `day: 5001` at tick 5000, but `STEPS_PER_DAY = 49`. Tick 5000 should be day ~102, not day 5001. The day counter appears to be incrementing 1:1 with ticks instead of using `STEPS_PER_DAY`.

## Fun Assessment

### Meaningful Player Agency: 1/5

**Player designations have zero observable impact.** In the interactive starvation test, designating a farm at (100,100) and a well at (105,100) at tick 0 produced the exact same death pattern as the baseline. Designating eat and drink tasks also made no difference. The outcome at tick 500 was identical (6 alive, 1 dead, same 3 in tantrum, same death).

Similarly, in the long-run-stability interactive test with 2 farms, a well, 3 beds, eat orders, and drink orders all placed at tick 0, the result was identical: all 7 dead by tick 1000, same causes, same order.

This suggests either:
- Designations are not being processed in headless mode
- Farm/well/forage/brew pipelines are too slow to prevent the initial resource crash
- Or dwarves simply cannot reach and use designated structures fast enough

**This is the single biggest problem.** A game where player decisions have no effect on outcomes is not fun.

### Difficulty: 0/5 (unfairly hard -- unwinnable)

Every scenario ends in TPK. There is no combination of starting resources or player actions that prevents total death. The game is not "hard" -- it is unwinnable in its current state.

### Interesting Events/Stories: 2/5

There are some emergent moments:
- The tantrum spiral (3 dwarves hit critical needs simultaneously and enter tantrums, compounding stress)
- Monster attacks picking off dwarves mid-sleep creates dramatic moments
- The overcrowding scenario shows that social need stays high with many dwarves (social 96-100), but physical needs still collapse

However, the `events_count` is always 0 and `recent_events` is always empty, so the game generates no narrative events. No artifacts, no strange moods, no friendships, no marriages -- none of the rich social systems fire in 500-5000 ticks.

### Pacing: 2/5

The decline from "everyone fine" to "everyone dead" happens between tick 200 and tick 800. That is roughly 60 seconds of real time at 10 ticks/second (`STEPS_PER_SECOND = 10`). This is too fast for the player to observe, react, and intervene, especially since interventions appear to have no effect.

## Bugs or Unexpected Behavior

1. **Day counter is 1:1 with ticks.** `day: 5001` at tick 5000 should be ~102 based on `STEPS_PER_DAY = 49`.

2. **Player designations have no observable effect.** Farm, well, bed, eat, and drink designations placed at tick 0 produce identical outcomes to scenarios with no player input. Either the task pipeline is broken in headless mode or structures take too long to build and produce food/drink.

3. **events_count is always 0.** Over 5000 ticks with 7 dwarves, no world events were generated. Strange moods (`STRANGE_MOOD_CHANCE = 0.05`), disease outbreaks (`DISEASE_OUTBREAK_CHANCE = 0.07`), and relationship formation (`FRIENDSHIP_FORMATION_CHANCE = 0.3`) should have triggered at least once. These systems may not be running in headless/scenario mode.

4. **Dead dwarves retain `is_in_tantrum: true`.** Several dead dwarves show tantrum state, which should be cleared on death.

5. **Need values freeze at death.** Dead dwarves show frozen need values (e.g., food=47, drink=36) rather than being zeroed or removed. This could confuse analysis.

6. **Personality traits are all null.** No dwarf in any scenario has personality traits set (`trait_openness: null`, etc.). The personality system (`NEUROTICISM_STRESS_MULTIPLIER`, `CONSCIENTIOUSNESS_WORK_MULTIPLIER`, etc.) is entirely inactive, meaning all dwarves behave identically.

## Suggestions

### Critical Priority (game is unwinnable without these)

1. **Fix the food/drink production pipeline.** Verify that auto-forage (`MIN_FORAGE_FOOD_STOCK`) and auto-brew (`MIN_DRINK_STOCK`) actually trigger and complete in time. Currently dwarves exhaust starting supplies and die before any production kicks in. Consider:
   - Increase `MIN_FORAGE_FOOD_STOCK` from 5 to 15
   - Increase `MIN_DRINK_STOCK` from 10 to 20
   - Add a starting food/drink buffer: scenarios should begin with enough resources for ~500 ticks minimum
   - Consider passive food/drink regeneration as a safety net

2. **Reduce early-game monster pressure.** `MONSTER_SPAWN_INTERVAL = 200` is too aggressive for a fortress with no military.
   - Increase to 500-1000 for the first in-game year
   - Or scale `MONSTER_SPAWN_INTERVAL` based on population size (fewer dwarves = less frequent spawns)
   - Or add a "peace period" of 500-1000 ticks before first monster spawn

3. **Verify player designations work in headless mode.** The fact that farms, wells, beds, eat/drink orders produce identical outcomes strongly suggests a bug in designation processing.

### High Priority (balance and fun)

4. **Slow down need decay rates.** Current rates make all needs hit "low" by tick 200, leaving no breathing room.
   - `FOOD_DECAY_PER_TICK`: reduce from 0.15 to 0.08 (dwarves eat roughly once per in-game day instead of twice)
   - `DRINK_DECAY_PER_TICK`: reduce from 0.2 to 0.12
   - `SLEEP_DECAY_PER_TICK`: reduce from 0.12 to 0.08
   - These changes would roughly double the time before dwarves hit critical needs

5. **Increase food/drink restore amounts.**
   - `FOOD_RESTORE_AMOUNT`: increase from 40 to 60
   - `DRINK_RESTORE_AMOUNT`: increase from 50 to 70
   - This makes each meal/drink last longer, reducing the total supply needed

6. **Assign personality traits to scenario dwarves.** Set `trait_*` values (even random ones from the RNG) so the personality system actually runs. Currently `scenarios.ts:87-91` sets all traits to `null`.

### Medium Priority (narrative and engagement)

7. **Fix day counter.** Use `Math.floor(tick / STEPS_PER_DAY)` instead of 1:1 tick-to-day mapping.

8. **Enable world events in scenario mode.** Strange moods, diseases, friendships, and other social systems should fire to create emergent stories. Over 5000 ticks, we should see at least a few events.

9. **Increase beauty passive restore** from `BEAUTY_RESTORE_PASSIVE = 0.02` to at least `0.03` to match `BEAUTY_DECAY_PER_TICK = 0.03`, so beauty stabilizes rather than monotonically declining.

10. **Add a "caravan rescue" mechanic.** `CARAVAN_INTERVAL_YEARS = 5` means 5 * 18000 = 90,000 ticks between caravans. At current lethality, no fortress survives past tick 1000. Either reduce the interval dramatically or tie caravan arrival to population alerts.

### Low Priority (quality of life)

11. **Clear tantrum state on death.** Dead dwarves should not report `is_in_tantrum: true`.

12. **Add more starting scenario variety.** All four scenarios have the same seed-based RNG and similar collapse patterns. Consider a "tutorial" scenario with drastically increased resources and no monster spawns.
